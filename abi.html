ABOUT ME

Biryani is a popular and flavorful mixed rice dish originating from the Indian subcontinent. It's made with aromatic basmati rice, meat (such as chicken, lamb,
beef, or fish), and a blend of spices like saffron, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, bay leaves, and cumin. Vegetarian versions often include vegetables like potatoes, carrots, and peas.
The rice and meat are cooked separately and then layered together, sometimes with yogurt, ghee (clarified butter), fried onions, and fresh herbs like mint and
cilantro. The dish is then cooked on low heat to let the flavors meld together. Biryani is known for its rich aroma and distinctive taste, and it is enjoyed across South Asia, the Middle East, and beyond, with regional variations like Hyderabadi, Kolkata, and Malabar biryanis.
